Steak

Description

Prepare to embark on a culinary journey to carnivorous perfection with our succulent Juicy Steak. This dish is a testament to the art of simplicity, where the focus is on the quality of the ingredients and the mastery of cooking techniques.

Whether you choose to grill your steak to smoky, outdoor perfection or sear it to a mouthwatering crust in a hot skillet, the result is an enticingly charred exterior that gives way to a juicy, tender interior. The aroma of sizzling beef fills the air, promising an exquisite culinary experience.

Ingredients

  • 2 boneless ribeye or New York strip steaks (about 1 inch thick)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 sprigs of fresh rosemary (or your preferred herbs)
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • Optional: Steak seasoning or rub of your choice (e.g., Montreal steak seasoning)

Steps

  1. Bring the Steak to Room Temperature:

    Remove the steaks from the refrigerator and let them sit at room temperature for about 30-45 minutes. This helps ensure even cooking.

  2. Preheat the Grill or Pan:
    If using a grill, preheat it to high heat (about 450-500°F/232-260°C). If using a stovetop, heat a heavy skillet (cast iron works well) over high heat until it's very hot.
  3. Season the Steaks:
    Drizzle the olive oil over both sides of the steaks and rub it in. Then, sprinkle minced garlic evenly over the steaks. Season both sides generously with salt, black pepper, and any steak seasoning or rub you prefer. Press the seasonings into the meat.
  4. Grill or Pan-Sear the Steaks:
    Place the steaks on the hot grill or in the hot skillet. If you're using a grill, close the lid. For medium-rare steaks, cook for about 4-5 minutes on each side (adjust the time based on your desired level of doneness). For pan-searing, use a pair of tongs to flip the steaks when they develop a nice sear, usually after 4-5 minutes. Cook for an additional 4-5 minutes on the other side.
  5. Rest the Steaks:
    Remove the steaks from the grill or skillet and place them on a cutting board or a warm plate. Tent them loosely with aluminum foil and let them rest for about 5-10 minutes. This resting period allows the juices to redistribute throughout the meat, ensuring a juicy steak.
  6. Slice and Serve:
    After resting, slice the steaks against the grain for maximum tenderness. Serve immediately, garnished with fresh rosemary sprigs or your choice of herbs.
