Boston Cream Pie

Description

Indulge in the timeless elegance of our Boston Cream Pie, a delectable dessert that marries the best of cake, custard, and chocolate into one unforgettable creation.

At first glance, the Boston Cream Pie is a sight to behold, with its golden-yellow cake layers that whisper of buttery indulgence. These tender, moist layers cradle a luxurious surprise—a luscious pastry cream filling that's velvety and rich. Every forkful of this cake is a harmonious blend of textures, where the softness of the cake meets the creamy embrace of the custard.

Ingredients

For the Yellow Cake Layers:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup (1 stick) unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup whole milk

For the Pastry Cream Filling:

  • 2 cups whole milk
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup cornstarch
  • 4 large egg yolks
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract

For the Chocolate Ganache Glaze:

  • 4 ounces semisweet chocolate, chopped
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter

Steps

For the Yellow Cake Layers:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour two 9-inch round cake pans.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt. Set aside.
  3. In a large mixing bowl, cream together the softened butter and granulated sugar until light and fluffy, which should take about 2-3 minutes.
  4. Beat in the eggs, one at a time, followed by the vanilla extract.
  5. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet mixture, alternating with the milk. Start and end with the dry ingredients, mixing until just combined after each addition.
  6. Divide the cake batter evenly between the prepared cake pans.
  7. Bake in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center of the cakes comes out clean.
  8. Let the cakes cool in the pans for about 10 minutes, then remove them from the pans and place them on a wire rack to cool completely.

For the Pastry Cream Filling:

  1. In a medium saucepan, heat the milk over medium heat until it begins to steam but is not boiling. Remove it from the heat and set it aside.
  2. In a separate mixing bowl, whisk together the sugar, cornstarch, and egg yolks until the mixture is pale and slightly thickened.
  3. Gradually whisk the warm milk into the egg mixture.
  4. Return the mixture to the saucepan and cook over medium heat, whisking constantly until it thickens and comes to a boil. This should take about 5-7 minutes.
  5. Remove the pastry cream from the heat and stir in the vanilla extract.
  6. Transfer the pastry cream to a bowl and cover it with plastic wrap, pressing the wrap directly against the surface of the cream to prevent a skin from forming.
  7. Refrigerate the pastry cream until it's chilled and set, which usually takes about 2 hours.

For the Chocolate Ganache Glaze:

  1. In a microwave-safe bowl or on the stovetop, heat the heavy cream until it's hot but not boiling.
  2. Add the chopped chocolate and butter to the hot cream and let it sit for a minute to soften.
  3. Stir the mixture until the chocolate and butter are completely melted and the ganache is smooth. Allow it to cool slightly.

Assembly:

  1. Place one of the cooled cake layers on a serving plate.
  2. Spread the chilled pastry cream evenly over the top of this layer.
  3. Place the second cake layer on top of the pastry cream.
  4. Pour the chocolate ganache over the top of the cake, allowing it to drip down the sides.
  5. Refrigerate the Boston Cream Pie for at least 1-2 hours to allow the ganache to set.
  6. Slice, serve, and enjoy your homemade Boston Cream Pie!
